Githeri

Ingredients

  • Maize (corn)
  • Beans (typically red kidney beans, but other varieties like green grams or cowpeas can be used)
  • Vegetables (such as potatoes, carrots, spinach, or kale)
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes (fresh or tomato paste)
  • Garlic and Ginger (optional)
  • Cooking oil
  • Spices (salt, pepper, curry powder, etc.)
  • Water or broth

Description

Githeri is a traditional Kenyan dish made by cooking maize (corn) and beans together, creating a hearty and nutritious meal. It is often accompanied by vegetables such as potatoes, carrots, and leafy greens like spinach or kale. The dish is flavored with onions, tomatoes, garlic, and ginger, and typically seasoned with spices like salt, pepper, and curry powder. Githeri can also include meat such as beef, chicken, or fish, depending on the variation. This comforting and versatile meal is popular for its balance of carbohydrates, protein, and vegetables, making it a staple in Kenyan cuisine.

Method

  1. Soak the Maize and Beans: Soak dried maize and beans overnight in water.
  2. Boil the Maize and Beans: Drain the soaked maize and beans, add them to a pot, cover with water, and boil for 1 to 1.5 hours until tender.
  3. Prepare the Vegetables: Peel and chop the potatoes and carrots. Chop the onions, tomatoes, garlic, and ginger.
  4. Sauté the Aromatics: Heat oil in a pan, sauté the onions until golden, then add garlic and ginger and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add Tomatoes and Spices: Add tomatoes (or tomato paste), salt, pepper, and optional spices like curry powder. Cook until the tomatoes soften.
  6. Add Potatoes and Carrots: Add the chopped potatoes and carrots to the boiled maize and beans, cover with water, and simmer for 20-30 minutes until tender.
  7. Combine with Aromatics: Stir the sautéed onion, tomato, and spice mixture into the maize and beans. Mix well.
  8. Add Greens (Optional): Add chopped spinach or kale and cook for 5-10 minutes until tender.
  9. Add Meat (Optional): If using meat, add cooked meat or cook it separately and add it to the pot.
  10. Simmer and Adjust Seasoning: Let everything simmer for 10 minutes, then taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  11. Serve: Serve the githeri hot and enjoy!
